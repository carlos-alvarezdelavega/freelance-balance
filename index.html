Freelance Balance

This study aims to understand the work-life balance of freelancers who use the platform Upwork. If you decide to take part, we will ask you to complete short activities on your mobile phone for 10 days with pre- and post- interviews. In this website, you will find all the necessary information about the study and how to participate. Please read all this information carefully. If you have any questions, you can always reach out to us at: carlos[at]freelbalance[dot]info



Who can take part?

Who can take part?

We are recruiting professionals that use the Upwork platform regularly. To take part, you must fulfil the following requirements:



  • Be 18 years or older.
  • Use the platform Upwork regularly (in the past six months or more) to conduct freelance work and have completed at least one job through the platform.
  • Intend to use the Upwork platform as a freelancer during the period of the study (upcoming months).

    • About the study

    We will ask you to take part in a 10-day study with pre- and post-interviews. During the study, you will complete short daily activities that involve:



  • Capturing aspects of your workday.
  • Sharing if you feel Upwork disrupts moments of your day.
  • Reflecting on how in control of you feel about your work-life balance.


    • You will use your smartphone to complete these activities and share them with the research team using WhatsApp. In detail, the study consists of the four stages below.



    1. Registration Survey

    Fill in a short survey with some information about you and your work. The survey shouldnâ€™t take more than 15 mins to complete. All information will be kept confidential.


    2. On-Boarding Interview

    Once you have completed the registration survey, we'll invite you to a short interview to explain the study activities. In the interview, the lead researcher will reiterate the purposes of the study and clarify any questions you might have. This interview will last between 15-30 minutes and will take place over Microsoft Teams or Skype at a time of your convenience.

    3. Work-Life Balance Activities

    Starting the day after the on-boarding interview, you will be asked to complete a series of short, daily activities for 10 days, which should not take you more than 5 minutes. The activities will consist of sharing your views on aspects of your day through WhatsApp. You will receive one activity prompt and one reminder per day on WhatsApp; you should complete the activities whenever it is most convenient for you, and not when you get the reminders. You can respond to these activities using different formats, such as taking photos, recording voice notes, filming yourself, or simply typing down your answers.

    * If you do not have access to WhatsApp, you can still take part in the study by sharing your activities via email.

    4. Follow-up Interview

    After completing the work-life balance activities, you will take part in a follow-up interview within a week from the end of the activities. The purpose of this interview is to talk through your experience with the study, provide more details, and more contextual explanations. The interview will last between 60-90 minutes and will take place over Microsoft Teams or Skype at a date and time of your convenience.

    FAQs



    Do I have to take part?

    No. Participation is entirely voluntary. If you do decide to take part, you can stop being involved in the study whenever you choose, without giving any reason. You are welcome to take part in the on-boarding interview and decide whether or not you want to participate in the subsequent stages. If you have further questions you can always reach out to the team at:

    carlos [at] freelbalance [dot] info



    Will I be compensated for taking part?

    Once you complete all study stages (survey, on-boarding interview, 12-day work-life balance activities, and follow-up interview), you will receive an Amazon voucher worth Â£30 as compensation for your time.



    How much time will I dedicate to the study?

    The study has a total duration of 12 days between activities and interviews. The activities should not take more than 5 minutes to complete across 10 days. The on-boarding interview should take between 20-30 minutes, while the follow-up interview should take between 60-90 minutes. We can accommodate the interviews to fit your schedule.

    Who is Organising and Funding the Study?

    This study is organised and funded by Northumbria University, Newcastle upon Tyne, UK. This study has no relation to the Upwork platform in any form.



    Will my taking part in this study be kept confidential and anonymous?

    Yes. Your name will not be written on any of the data we collect; the written information you provide will have an ID number â€“ not your name. Your name will not be written on the recorded interviews, or on the typed up versions of your discussions from the interview, and your name will not appear in any reports or documents resulting from this study. All the data collected from you in this study will be confidential, taking especial care of the information you share through the activities. The only exception to this confidentiality is if the researcher feels that you or others may be harmed if information is not shared.
